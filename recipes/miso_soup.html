Miso Soup

Description:

Miso soup is a traditional Japanese soup made from a base of dashi (a savory broth) and miso paste, typically served with tofu, seaweed, and green onions. It's a light, flavorful dish that's easy to prepare and often enjoyed as part of a Japanese meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dashi (Japanese soup stock) – you can use instant dashi powder or make your own
  • 2 tbsp miso paste (red or white, based on your preference)
  • 1/2 cup tofu (cubed)
  • 1/4 cup dried seaweed (wakame)
  • 1-2 green onions (chopped)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Dashi: If you're using instant dashi powder, dissolve 1 teaspoon of dashi powder in 2 cups of hot water. Stir until fully dissolved. If you're using homemade dashi, simply heat the stock until it’s warm.
  2. Add Miso Paste: In a small bowl, take a bit of the warm dashi and mix it with the miso paste until smooth and dissolved. This prevents clumps in the soup.
  3. Add Tofu and Seaweed: Add the cubed tofu and dried seaweed (wakame) to the pot. Stir gently and let it simmer for about 3-4 minutes to heat through.
  4. Finish with Green Onions: Add the chopped green onions to the soup right before serving for a fresh, crunchy bite.
  5. Serve: Ladle the miso soup into bowls and enjoy!
