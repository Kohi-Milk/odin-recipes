Yakitori

Description:

Yakitori is a popular Japanese dish consisting of skewered, grilled chicken pieces, often seasoned with salt (shio) or a savory sauce (tare) made from soy sauce, sake, and mirin. The chicken is typically cut into bite-sized pieces, and various parts of the bird, such as thighs, breasts, skin, and liver, may be used. Yakitori is commonly served as a street food or in izakayas (Japanese pubs), offering a delicious and smoky flavor from the grill.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken thighs
  • 1-2 green onions (optional)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt (for seasoning)
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the chicken: Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces (around 1-2 inch chunks). If using green onions, cut them into 1-inch pieces.
  2. Make the tare sauce: In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, sake, mirin, and sugar. Bring it to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves. Let it simmer for about 5-10 minutes to thicken slightly. Remove from heat.
  3. Skewer the chicken: Thread the chicken pieces (and green onions, if using) onto the soaked bamboo skewers, alternating between the chicken and green onions.
  4. Grill the yakitori: Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and has a slight char. Brush with the tare sauce during the last 2-3 minutes of grilling, turning the skewers to coat evenly.
  5. Serve: Remove from the grill and sprinkle with a pinch of salt (if desired). Serve hot with extra tare sauce on the side.
